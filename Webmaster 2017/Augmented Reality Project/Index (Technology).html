Definition, Explanation, and History

"Augmented reality is a live direct or indirect view of a physical, real world environment whose elements are augmented (or supplemented) by computer-generated sensory input such as sound, video, graphics, or GPS data" -Wikipedia entry for Augmented reality.

The way it functions is based on visual sensors and trackers, which are used to track depths and solid objects in the environment. The program used than displays the data desired through a device, whether it involves seeing through it, such as mobile devices,head mounted displays, glasses, and lenses; or simply viewing a projected image already in the environment. Many have interactivity whether it be through a device, gesture tracking, or even neural signals.

In 1968, Ivan Sutherland pioneered a head-mounted display system which used computer-generated graphics to display wireframe drawings. in 1974 Myron Krueger constructed a 'laboratory' called Videoplace, which paired projectors with video cameras which displayed onscreen silhouettes. The term "Augmented Reality" was coined by Boeing researcher Tom Caudell in 1990. From 1998 to 2009, uses for augmented reality included first down markers during NFL games on television, the naval Battlefield Augmented Reality System, NASA overlaying map data for greater flight navigation, and ARToolkit bringing augmented reality to web browsers. Today we have products such as the Volkswagen (Mobile Augmented Reality Technical Assistance) MARTA app, Google glass, and the Microsoft Hololens Developer Kit. In 2016, augmented reality and virtual reality investment reached $1.1 billion.
