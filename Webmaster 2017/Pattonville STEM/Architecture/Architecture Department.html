Manufacturing Processes

Darrell Langston

Jonathon Saettelle

An introductory course to the world of manufacturing. Students learn to meet a specified criteria for a project with the help of technical drawing. The safe and efficient use of tools in design and manufacturing are also given importance. Specific topics covered include technical sketching, the Customary and Metric systems, geometric construction, ortographic projection, types of saw, and types of finishes.

Computer Aided Drafting 1 and 2

Darrell Langston

Jonathon Saettelle

Implementing the use of Computer Aided Drafting software, students gain an understanding of topics ranging from the Cartesian Coordinate System as it pertains to drafting, further geometric construction, pictorial drawings, intersections and development, threads, fasteners, and springs, the types of gearing and cams, and three-dimensional along with parametric modeling.

Specialized Drafting

Darrell Langston

Designed to provide in-depth experiences such as descriptive geometry, technical drawing, architectural drawing and tool design. Since this is an independent study type course, the student shall identify the problem they want to work on and provide an outline for their progress throughout the semester. The student will be given specialized drafting technology at the beginning of the semester if space is available.

Architectural Drawing

Darrell Langston

Units of this courses curriculum fall upon lessons regarding the theory of design, living areas, service area, sleeping area, techniques, electrical plans, architectural work drawings, building methods and materials, required sizes, schedules, and building codes. The student shall draw and design a home and will be given assignments in blue-print reading.
